About

I've dabbled in programming since I was ten, hacking together crappy choose-your-own-adventure games in BASIC on my parents' Apple IIe. These days I get a kick out of working with Lisp, Python, and Javascript. I don't have a degree in CS, but I took enough CS courses in University to give me a solid foundation.

FreeCodeCamp appeals to me because it gives me a public, community driven course that'll push me to brush up on my web development skills and actually complete a few simple projects. I'm also looking forward to being able to help non-profits with their web projects.

I'm also a fiction writer: I hold an MFA in Creative Writing from UBC, and was the recipient of a Gold National Magazine Award for fiction in 2010. My work has appeared most recently in Event, Vancouver Review, and the Machine of Death Anthology.

I'm currently a Senior Technical Project Manager at Hootsuite. I work with our Product and Software Development teams to deliver large-scale projects and act as an Agile Coach and guide.

Other interests? I'm a craft beer enthusiast and homebrewer, and a runner of marathons & half-marathons.
